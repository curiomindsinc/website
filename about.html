About Me

I am a passionate Biology educator dedicated to making science engaging, meaningful, and accessible for every learner. As a teacher, I strive to bridge scientific concepts with real-world experiences through inquiry-based learning, interactive simulations, hands-on activities, and technology-enhanced lessons. I believe that students learn best when they are curious, actively involved, and given opportunities to explore and question the world around them.


That same belief is what led me to create CurioMinds.inc — a space where those simulations and digital resources can reach students far beyond my own classroom. I wanted to build tools that are visual, interactive, and genuinely fun, so that any student, anywhere, can experience the curiosity and wonder that science deserves to inspire. My goal is to nurture not just academic performance, but critical thinking, creativity, and a lifelong appreciation for science — and CurioMinds.inc is my way of extending that mission to every learner I may never meet in person.

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy is grounded in the belief that learning should be active, engaging, and student-centred. I believe every student is capable of growth when provided with the right support, encouragement, and opportunities to explore concepts in meaningful ways. As an educator, my role is not only to deliver content, but also to spark curiosity, build confidence, and cultivate independent thinkers.

In science education, I emphasise inquiry, exploration, and real-world application. I aim to create lessons that encourage students to ask questions, investigate ideas, and connect scientific concepts to everyday life. By incorporating technology, simulations, collaborative activities, and hands-on learning experiences, I strive to make abstract concepts more accessible and enjoyable.

I also believe that a positive classroom culture is essential for effective learning. Students should feel safe to make mistakes, share ideas, and challenge themselves. I seek to foster a classroom environment built on respect, curiosity, and resilience, where students are motivated to take ownership of their learning.

Ultimately, I hope to nurture scientifically literate individuals who are not only knowledgeable, but also curious about the world, thoughtful in their decision-making, and confident in solving problems creatively and critically.
