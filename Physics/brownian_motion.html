🔬 Brownian Motion Simulation

Brownian motion occurs because tiny air molecules constantly collide with larger particles such as pollen grains, causing random motion.

Created by: Mr Lau Chin Hang | Instagram: @curiominds.inc

Simulation

Air Molecules - Fast-moving particles
Pollen Grain - Larger particle exhibiting random motion
💡 What you're seeing: Hundreds of air molecules bombard the pollen grain from all directions. The unbalanced collisions cause the pollen to move randomly - this is Brownian motion, first observed by Robert Brown in 1827!

🌡️ Temperature Effect: Higher temperature means faster-moving air molecules, which causes more energetic Brownian motion. This demonstrates the direct relationship between temperature and molecular kinetic energy!
